# Report: “AWS First Cloud AI Journey - Community Day”

### Event Objectives

- Create opportunities for young people to practice presentation skills and share knowledge.
- Update practical knowledge on career mindset (workplace mindset).
- Explore methods for self-motivating effective learning.
- Learn about the application of cloud computing (AWS) and artificial intelligence (AI) in Prompt Engineering and software development.

### Speakers

- **Huynh Hoang Long** - Admin of FCAJ
- **Nguyen Tuan Thinh** - DevOps/Cloud Engineer First Cloud AI Journey
- **Mr. Khang** - Solutions Architect, Cloud Kinetics
- **Ms. Thao** - Software Developer, VIB International Bank

### Key Highlights

#### 1. "Brain-hacking" for learning motivation

- Analyze why the brain easily gets "addicted" to social media/games (Dopamine, instant gratification, curiosity).
- Offer solutions to trick the brain, such as: drawing random rewards after learning, leveraging the fear of "losing a streak", and breaking down knowledge to avoid overwhelm.
- Apply the "2-minute rule" to overcome procrastination.

#### 2. Ultimate Prompt Engineering & AWS Serverless

This session guides on how to communicate effectively with AI and build supporting systems:
- 7 elements to write precise prompts (Role, Instruction, Context, Input, Output Format, Constraints).
- Demo of the "Prompt Optimizer" application built on AWS infrastructure (CloudFront, S3, API Gateway, Lambda, Bedrock, DynamoDB).

#### 3. Working mindset in the AI era

The sharing session focused on mindset orientation for Fresher/Junior:
- AI does not replace humans; it only "Amplifies" capability: doing well will multiply productivity by 10, doing poorly will create worse products.
- Emphasize the importance of foundational knowledge and the mindset of continuously asking "Why?".
- Value the integrity and courage to face mistakes.

#### 4. BMX Software Development Method (Through Method for AI Development)

Introduce the AI application process to avoid **hallucination** when coding large projects:
- **3 integration patterns**: Publish/Subscribe, Point-to-point, Streaming
- **Benefits**: Loose coupling, scalability, resilience
- **Comparison between sync vs async**: Understanding the trade-offs

### What I Learned

- Understand the mechanism of Dopamine to build a self-reward system and maintain learning habits.
- Know how to structure a complex command (prompt) to make the most of LLMs.
- Clearly realize: Do not "outsource" your own thinking to AI. You must thoroughly understand the nature of the work instead of completely relying on AI-generated results.
- Practical perspective from employers: Value honesty, attitude, and alignment with long-term goals (experience, network) over starting salary.

### Event Experience

The event brought a multidimensional perspective, excellently combining soft skills (psychology, career orientation) and hard skills (AWS, Prompt Engineering, software processes). The event environment was highly encouraging, reflecting the true spirit of an open technology community.

#### Networking and Exchange
- Listened to real-world experience and sincere advice from seniors.
- Recognized the common pain points of IT students today and how the community is helping each other overcome them.
- Motivated myself to be more proactive in self-studying and ready to share knowledge with others.


> Overall, this was a highly practical and inspiring meetup that not only provided technical knowledge but also equipped students with the mindset and attitude needed to step firmly on their future career path.
